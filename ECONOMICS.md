Unit Economics & Mathematical Feasibility

This section models the financial viability of Credex as a funnel-driven SaaS distribution layer. The Audit Engine is not just a tool — it is a lead qualification system that converts “AI spend inefficiency” into monetizable enterprise savings opportunities.

💰 Value of a Converted Lead
Target customer: Series A/B startup (~50 engineers)
Typical optimized AI stack: ~30–40 paid seats across tools like Cursor, Copilot, Claude, ChatGPT
Average blended retail pricing:
~$30 per seat/month
~30 seats average usage baseline

Annualized tool spend:

$30 × 30 × 12 = $10,800 / year

If Credex negotiates/aggregates usage into discounted enterprise credit pools, we estimate:

Net gross margin per converted customer: ~$2,500 – $3,500 ARR
Conservative assumption used: $3,000 ARR per converted logo
📊 Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

This funnel is intentionally designed to be distribution-heavy and product-led.

Organic channels (HN, X/Twitter, GitHub): $0 paid spend
Infra cost (LLM + API calls): negligible (~<$1 per 1000 audits)
Engineering + maintenance amortized across funnel

Effective blended CAC estimate: ~$50 per qualified lead

🔁 Conversion Funnel Breakdown
Top-of-funnel audits completed: ~20,000 companies/year
Email capture conversion: ~10% → ~2,000 leads
Qualified high-intent leads (>$500/mo savings detected): ~15% → ~300 MQLs
Close rate (value-aligned + quantified savings): ~50% → ~150 customers
📈 Revenue Projection
150 closed deals × $3,000 ARR per deal
= ~$450,000 ARR (Year 1 projection)

This represents only organic funnel performance without paid scaling.
